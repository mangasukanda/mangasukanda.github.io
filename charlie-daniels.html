Career

In 1964, Daniels co-wrote “It Hurts Me” (a song that Elvis Presley recorded) with his friend, producer Bob Johnston, under the name of Bob’s wife, Joy Byers. He was a Nashville session musician. His first hit, the novelty song “Uneasy Rider,” came from his third album, Honey in the Rock, in 1973, to No. 9 on the Billboard Hot 100. Daniels was honored as a BMI Icon at the 53rd annual BMI Country Awards on October 18, 2005. Daniels lived in Mount Juliet, Tennessee, where the town named the park after him.
