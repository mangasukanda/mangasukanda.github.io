His community activism began in Chicago in the 1930s. Early in Barack Obama‘s first presidential campaign, the soon-to-be-president’s association with the radical leader, Alinsky, was mentioned. He put aside racial politics to unite black and white workers during the Great Depression. His early community organization efforts took place in Chicago’s Back of the Yards and Woodlawn neighborhoods.
