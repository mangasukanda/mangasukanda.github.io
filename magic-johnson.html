Magic Johnson Age & Early Life

Magic Johnson was born in Lansing, Michigan on August 14, 1959, to parents Earvin Johnson Sr. and Christine Johnson. Earvin Johnson Jr. is his given name. Earvin, his father, used to work as an assembly worker at General Motors. Pearl, Quincy, Larry, Lois, Yvonne, and Kim are his six siblings. Evelyn, Mary, and Michael Johnson are his half-siblings.

When it comes to his youth, he was profoundly inspired by her parents’ work in ethics. He also used to assist her folks by collecting rubbish. As a result, his neighborhood kids used to refer to him as Garbage Man. At the same time, he became obsessed with basketball. He also admired the basketball star, Bill Russell. Johnson became one of the most formidable players in junior high when he was in eighth grade. He afterward enrolled at Everett High School. He did, however, aspire to attend Sexton High School. During his sophomore season, he established a record with a triple-double of 36 points, 18 rebounds, and 16 assists. He led his High School to state title games in his last year, going 27-1.

Career

After graduating from high school, he attended Michigan State University in East Lansing to pursue a career as a television analyst. He played alongside Greg Kelser, Jay Vicent, and Mike Berkovich at college. He finished the season as a freshman with an average of 17.0 points, 7.9 rebounds, and 7.4 assists. Johnson was picked in the 1979 NBA draft after two years of college with averages of 17.1 points, 7.6 rebounds, and 7.9 assists per game.

Magic Johnson was picked by the Los Angeles Lakers in 1979. He was teammates with Kareem Abdul-Jabbar at the time. He averaged 18 points, 7.7 rebounds, and 7.3 assists per game for the NBS All-Rookie Team. Johnson missed 45 games the following season due to torn cartilage. Following that, he signed a 25-year deal with the Lakers for $25 million, making him the highest-paid player in history at the time. During the 1982-83 NBQ season, he averaged 16.8 points, 8.6 rebounds, and 10.5 assists per game. During the season, he was named to the All-NBA First Team for the first time. Laker reached the finals for the third time in a row the following season. However, the squad fell 124-121 because Johnson couldn’t get the shot off before the final buzzer.

In the 1985-86 season, he averaged 23.9 points, 12.2 assists, and 6.3 rebounds per game, setting a career-best. During the season, he was named MVP in the final. The next year, he finished the season with an average of 19.6 points and 6.2 rebounds per game. He missed 10 games last season due to a groin ailment. He became one of the NBA’s most powerful players over time. During the 1991-92 NBA season, he tested positive for HIV. On November 7, 1991, he announced his retirement in a public statement. Despite his retirement, he was picked to play for the United States basketball team at the 1992 Summer Olympics in Barcelona.
