How did Clifton Collins Jr start his Professional Career?

In 1988, he started acting, appearing in small parts of movies. In honor of his grandfather Pedro Gonzalez Gonzalez, he changed his screen name to Clifton Gonzales-Gonzales two years later. The latter appeared in multiple John Wayne westerns in the 1950s and 1960s as a comic sidekick.

Clifton strove to avoid conventional “barrio” roles at the start of his career, much like his grandfather, who was notorious for playing negative Latino clichés in movies. He started as an urban gangster and a struggling blue-collar worker. Clifton portrayed Nino Gomez in the sci-fi futuristic prison drama “Fortress” and Tack in the comedy “The Stoned Age,” which had a ’70s theme.

He put a lot of effort into bucking the Latino stereotype, and his breakthrough came in the 1997 crime drama “One Eight Seven,” in which he acted as tagger César Sánchez alongside Samuel L. Jackson.

He was given the lead part of Martinez in Ray Bradbury’s cult classic “The Wonderful Ice Cream Suit” the following year. In the ensemble cast crime drama “Traffic,” directed by Steven Soderbergh, he portrays gay hitman Francisco “Frankie Flowers” Flores.
