Biography

James Watt was born in Greenock, Scotland on January 19, 1736. Scientist and entrepreneur who popularized the first steam engines, which had an integral effect on the course of the Industrial Revolution. He also developed the term horsepower as a measurement of power and the SI unit of power is named after him because of it. The British philosopher Aldous Huxley wrote that in inventing the steam engine, he also invented time as we think of it today. He served as an apprentice to a mathematical instrument maker but quickly outgrew his tutor in knowledge.
