Biography

Radney Foster was born in Del Rio, TX on July 20, 1959. American country singer and songwriter who is one-half of the musical duo Foster & Lloyd. He has released singles, including “Nobody Wins.” His song “I’m In” was recorded by Keith Urban for the 2009 album, Defying Gravity. He started playing guitar when he was twelve years old and was later a member of Phi Gamma Delta at the University of the South in Tennessee. He dropped out in 1979 to pursue a career in music.

Career

Foster started his solo career in 1992 and his album Del Rio, TX 1959, produced four consecutive Top 40 hits. However, his commercial success declined with the release of later albums such as Labor of Love (1995), See What You Want to See (1999), Are You Ready for the Big Show? Another Way to Go (2002) and This World We Live In (2006). Overall, Foster had thirteen songs on the Billboard Hot Country Songs charts, including the Top Ten hits “Just Call Me Lonesome” (#10, 1992) and “Nobody Wins” (#2, 1993). His songs were recorded by Gary Allan, Sara Evans, Keith Urban, Hootie and Blowfish, and Jack Ingram.
