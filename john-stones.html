John Stones is a typical Pep Guardiola central defender. His ball playing skills and composure in the heart of defensive line has made other established managers such as Jose Mourinho and Ronald Koeman his fans. He was one of the first signings in Guardiola’s City Revolution in the 2016 summer. The England National team coach Gareth Southgate also considers him a key part of the national setup and he started all of England’s matches in 2018 World Cup.

    First Soccer Match

    In March 2012, he made his professional debut with Barnsley in their Championship match against Reading. He was sent in the 52nd minute in his side’s 4-0 mauling.

    In August 2013, Stones made his first start for Everton FC in the second round League Cup match against Stevenage. His side won the match in the extra time by 2-1.

    In August 2016, he made his first appearance for Manchester City in their match against Sunderland. It was also Pep Guardiola’s first competitive match as City’s new manager.

    In May 2014, he made his first start for the English national team in a friendly match against Peru at the Wembley Stadium.

    John Stones Facts

  • In January 2013, he moved to Everton as the Merseyside club paid around £3 million to secure his services from Barnsley. He signed a five and a half years contract with the club.
  • In August 2014, he agreed on a contract extension with Everton to extend his contract with the club to 2019. The new contract deal also increased his earning to around £30,000 a week.
  • On the back of his impressive performances in Everton’s backline, he managed to earn a nomination for Golden Boy 2014 Award. However, he wasn’t able to overcome eventual winner Raheem Sterling.
  • In April 2015, he scored his first premier league goal for Everton as he headed in his side’s second goal in their 3-0 victory over Manchester United.
  • In the summer of 2015, he was actively pursued by Jose Mourinho‘s Chelsea. Everton reportedly rejected three bids (£20 million, £26 million, and £30 million) from the London based club. It was even claimed that Stones had submitted a transfer request to force a move. But the report was denied by the club and his representatives.
  • In August 2016, Everton agreed a £47.5 million deal with Manchester City to allow Stones to eventually leave the club. Everton was also promised further £2.5 million in performance and appearances related add-ons.
  • His old club Barnsley also received £6.78 million from his move to Abu Dhabi owned club because of the 15% sell on clause inserted during his move to Everton.
  • His transfer move to Man City made him the second defender after David Luiz to be sold for over £45 million. He was also the second most expensive defender at that time.
  • His debut season for Manchester City wasn’t up to the mark as he was regularly criticized for his role in his side’s shambolic performances throughout the season.
  • He scored 2 goals in England’s 6-1 annihilation of Panama in the 2018 FIFA World Cup group stage match. His goals made him the first Manchester City player since Trevor Francis in 1982 to score goals for the England National team.
