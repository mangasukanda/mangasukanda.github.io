Publish date: 2024-06-19

Despite its young age, skateboarding has already made an impact on extreme sports. The International Olympic Committee (IOC) has officially recognized skateboarding as an Olympic sport. Once tied to the streets of a beach town in California, the sport has gained global popularity and is expected to make its debut in Tokyo this summer. In fact, the sport is expected to become a part of the summer Olympics 2020. Its popularity has spread beyond its roots, but the IOC hopes to attract younger fans and engage young people in competitive sport.

Jay McLaughlin is an inspiration to young skateboarders. He is an Olympian and has defied the odds to make skateboarding a worldwide phenomenon. He demonstrates pure excellence in his attitude and in his sport, and he is a great role model for skateboarding. His wife Cynthia, meanwhile, is more cautious, enjoying the sport as a spectator. She admires the athletes for their talent, skill, and stamina.

The competitive aspect of skateboarding has spawned a whole new set of tricks, dubbed ollies. In skateboarding, the “ollie” is the foundation for most jumps, and a skater who can execute it successfully can shoot up to sixty feet in the air. The “ollie” is a hand-free aerial that was invented by Alan Gelfand in 1978. It is considered one of the hardest tricks in the world, but is very exciting to watch.
