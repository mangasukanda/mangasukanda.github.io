Who Is David Koch’s Wife: Meet Julia Koch. Julia Koch is a socialite and philanthropist from the United States. After her husband David Koch passed away, she became the owner of 42% of Koch Industries.

Julia Koch Biography

On April 12, 1962, Julia Margaret Flesher was born. Although her parents, Margaret and Frederic Flesher, had a furniture company named Flesher’s when she was born, her family had been farmers.

She spent her early years in Indianola, Iowa, before moving with her family to Arkansas when she was eight years old. There, her parents opened a clothes company there named Peggy Frederic’s, which she described as “a gorgeous, beautiful establishment.” Her father had returned to Indianola by 1998, while her mother was still living in Conway.

Flesher travelled to New York City in 1984 after earning her degree from the University of Central Arkansas and working as a model. There, she assisted fashion designer Adolfo and fitted Nancy Reagan.

In January 1991, she went on a blind date with David Koch, but the encounter did not go well. “I’m pleased I met that guy because now I know I never want to go out with him,” she said later when she was asked how she felt.

Later that year, however, they reconnected at a party and began dating. They were married in May 1996 at David Koch’s Southampton home on Meadow Lane after she ceased working there in 1993.

She made what the New York Times referred to as her “New York social debut” at the Met Gala in December 1997. Along with Anna Wintour and Patrick McCarthy, she served as co-chair of the event in that particular year.

She is “one of those individuals that happen in New York every few years,” according to McCarthy. She is gorgeous, loves fashion, is a skilled entertainer, and is married to a very wealthy guy.
