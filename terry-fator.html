Career

Terry Fator has played in two different bands. He served as the band’s lead vocalist for 1987 and 1988. The ensemble had performed in more than 200 high schools in Canada and the United States, averaging three performances per school day. The Young American Showcase was the producer.

Second, he was a member of a band in Texas, where he also sang lead. When he joined it at 23, he added T. Airedale, his puppet, to the program.

Terry entered America’s Got Talent competition after realizing that his celebrity was not as great as he had assumed. On June 19, 2007, he made his first national appearance on the program. He finally triumphed.

Fator also provides character voices. After combining all his skills—including ventriloquism, impersonations, singing, and comedy—he found career success. He kept performing at events after America’s Got Talent. On May 13, 2008, he appeared on The Oprah Winfrey Show.
