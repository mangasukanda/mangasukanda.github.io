Biography of Russell Simmons

Russell Wendell Simmons was born on October 4, 1957, in Queens, New York City. He grew up alongside his two brothers and was raised by his mother, a New York City park administrator, after his father left when he was only ten years old.

Simmons attended City College of New York but dropped out to pursue a career in the music industry. In 1979, he co-founded Def Jam Recordings with Rick Rubin, which went on to become one of the most influential hip-hop labels of all time. The label played a pivotal role in the emergence of artists like LL Cool J, Public Enemy, and the Beastie Boys.

In addition to his work with Def Jam Recordings, Simmons has also been involved in various business ventures over the years. He founded the clothing lines Phat Farm and Argyleculture, both of which were highly successful. Simmons has also dabbled in film production, co-founding Def Pictures in the 1990s.

In recent years, Simmons has become an advocate for various social and political causes. He is a supporter of animal rights and veganism and even founded the charity Rush Philanthropic Arts Foundation to support arts education in underserved communities. Simmons has also been a vocal advocate for criminal justice reform and has publicly supported the Black Lives Matter movement.

Throughout his career, Simmons has received numerous accolades, including inductions into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the NAACP Image Awards Hall of Fame. As of 2023, his net worth was estimated to be around $350 million.
