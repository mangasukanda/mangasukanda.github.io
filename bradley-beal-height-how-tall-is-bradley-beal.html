Bradley Beal height-American basketball player, Bradley Emmanuel Beal Sr. was born on June 28, 1993.

Beal was born in St. Louis, Missouri in the United States of America. In St. Louis, Missouri, Beal attended Chaminade College Preparatory School.

He represented the US at the 2010 FIBA Under-17 World Championship, where he helped his team win the title by scoring an average of 18 points per game.

Beal not only won the competition but also made the All-Tournament Team and took home the MVP prize. Beal averaged 32.5 points per game, 5.7 rebounds per game, and 2.8 assists per game in his senior year of high school.

Beal was selected the 2011 Mr. Show-Me Basketball at the conclusion of his senior year, recognizing him as the best high school basketball player in the state of Missouri. He received the 2011 Gatorade National Player of the Year award as well.

Bradley Beal career

Beal gave notice of his intent to enter the NBA draft on April 13, 2012, bypassing his remaining three years of college eligibility. Beal was selected by the W ashington Wizards with the third overall choice in the 2012 NBA Draft on June 28, 2012.

Beal set a new career best on November 10, 2013, when he scored 34 points in an overtime loss against the Oklahoma City Thunder.

Later, in a loss against the Memphis Grizzlies, he scored a career-high 37 points, which became the bar for his personal best at the time.

Beal had an MRI on October 11, 2014, which found a non-displaced fracture of the scaphoid bone in his left wrist, necessitating surgery.

Beal re-signed with the Wizards on July 26, 2016. He contributed 34 points to a 114-111 loss to the Miami Heat on November 19, 2016.

He scored a then-career-high 42 points in a 106-101 victory over the Phoenix Suns two days later, marking the first time in his five-year career that he had 30 or more in back-to-back games.

He had his fourth 40-point game of the year on February 24, 2017, as the Philadelphia 76ers defeated him 120-112.

With a game-high 33 points in a 92-91 victory over the Boston Celtics in Game 6 of their second-round playoff series on May 12, 2017, Beal enabled the Wizards to avoid going home and force a decisive Game 7.

Beal had a then-season-high 40 points on November 1, 2017, but the Washington Wizards lost to the Phoenix Suns 122-116. Beal scored 23 points and reached the milestone of 700 made 3-pointers in NBA history on November 20, 2017, in a 99-88 victory over the Milwaukee Bucks.

Beal contributed 17 of his 39 points in the fourth quarter in Washington’s 114-110 victory over the Chicago Bulls on December 31, 2017.

Beal was selected as an NBA All-Star for the first time on January 23, 2018. He scored 41 points in a 121–112 loss to the Oklahoma City Thunder two days later.

On October 20, 2018, Beal scored 32 points in a 117-113 loss to the Toronto Raptors while going 6 for 11 from beyond the arc. He surpassed Gilbert Arenas (868) to set a new record for the most 3-pointers made in a career for Washington by sinking his 869th in the fourth quarter.

Beal extended his deal with the Wizards for a maximum of $72 million over a two-year period on October 17, 2019.

Beal recorded a then-career-high 53 points on February 23, 2020, against the Chicago Bulls, despite the Wizards losing 126–117. Beal also overtook Jeff Malone to move into second place on the Wizards’ all-time scoring record during the contest.

On January 6, 2021, Beal tied Gilbert Arenas’ Wizards club record for points scored in a game by an individual by scoring 60 points, a career high, in a 141-136 loss to the Philadelphia 76ers.

On June 30, 2022, Beal had a $36.4 million option to leave the team and become a free agent. On July 6, he later agreed to a new 5-year, $251 million contract.

On October 21, Beal led the Washington Wizards to a 102-100 victory over the Chicago Bulls with 19 points and the game-winning bank shot. Beal had a season-high 37 points on February 28, 2023, as the Washington Wizards defeated the Atlanta Hawks 119-116.
